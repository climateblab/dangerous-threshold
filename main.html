Explore Mann's climate projections from Scientific American

Mann issued a dire warning of climate crisis, perhaps only two decades away: "[http://www.scientificamerican.com/article/mann-why-global-warming-will-cross-a-dangerous-threshold-in-2036, Why Global Warming Will Cross a Dangerous Threshold in 2036]" (Scientific American, April 2014). You can reproduce his evidence here to interactively explore various scenarios. The simulation runs in the browser.
To zoom/pan use the green "brush" control on the small graph. Click here to zoom into "faux pause".
  • *Historical data* (white): historical mean average temperature (1850--2013).
  • *Projection* (orange): assumes the world keeps burning fossil fuels at current rate.
  • *Stabilized* (light brown): assumes atmospheric CO2 levels are stabilized.
  • *Danger threshold* (red): 2-degree rise that scientists say would cause dangerous harm to human civilization.
  • *ECS* is __Equilibrium Climate Sensitivity__, which represents how sensitive the atmosphere is to the heating effect of greenhouse gases. The [http://www.ipcc.ch/,IPCC] estimates that the ECS is in the range *1.5--4.5*.
  • *CO2* is the atmospheric carbon dioxide level. In 2013, this was in the range *393--400 ppm*.
  • *Aerosols*. Aerosol particles in the atmosphere partly block the sun's heat. Checking this box (default) models a drop in current aerosol levels. Otherwise, the current level persists. (Interestingly, reducing coal burning decreases both CO2 emissions __and__ aerosol levels---see article.)
